How to Play

Basketball is a team sport. Commonly with 5 players on a team. On a rectangular cout. The goal is to score on the opponent basket. while preventing the opposite team to score on your basket. A field goal is worth two points, unless made from behind the three-point line, when it is worth three. After a foul, timed play stops and the player fouled or designated to shoot a technical foul is given one, two or three one-point free throws. The team with the most points at the end of the game wins, but if regulation play expires with the score tied, an additional period of play (overtime) is mandated.

Players advance the ball by bouncing it while walking or running (dribbling) or by passing it to a teammate, both of which require considerable skill. On offense, players may use a variety of shots â€“ the layup, the jump shot, or a dunk; on defense, they may steal the ball from a dribbler, intercept passes, or block shots; either offense or defense may collect a rebound, that is, a missed shot that bounces from rim or backboard. It is a violation to lift or drag one's pivot foot without dribbling the ball, to carry it, or to hold the ball with both hands then resume dribbling.

Shooting Foul

Shooting fouls are called when the defender has contact with an offensive player when the offensive player is shooting. The consequence of a shooting fouls are free throws. The amount of free throws awarded to the offensive player is based on the worth of the shot taken. If the shot is made when the offensive player is fouled, the the offensive player is awarded the points form the shot and an extra freethrow

Reaching Foul

Reaching Fouls are called when the defender has excesive contact with the ball-handler when trying to poke the ball from the ball-handler's hands. the consequence of the foul is reseting of the posession.

Blocking Foul

Blocking Fouls are called when the defender is excesive in the contact while blocking the path of the defender.

Charging Fouls

Charging fouls are called when the offensive player creates excesive contact with the deffensive player. the consequence is turning over the ball to the opposing team
